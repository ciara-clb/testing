Submit a Ticket — The Only Channel

Every break/fix issue must have a Zendesk ticket. This is the only channel through which LinearB Customer Support will formally track, triage, and resolve your requests against SLA commitments.

Note Your CSA (Customer Solutions Architect) and CSM (Customer Success Manager) are not responsible for the day-to-day tracking, triage, or resolution of break/fix tickets. While your CSM might mention the status of high-priority items in a weekly report, ticket ownership stays with LinearB Customer Support and you.

What We Need From You

To help us triage and resolve the issue quickly, we rely on you providing enough detail when you submit the ticket. Please include everything relevant to the problem, such as:

  • Screenshots or screen recordings
  • Browser console output
  • Affected URLs
  • Links to Pull Requests or Merge Requests
  • Project management ticket IDs
  • Affected user identifiers
  • A clear description of what you expected to happen versus what you actually observed
Important If we have to ask for missing information, our official response timeline (SLA) will be paused until you provide those details in the Zendesk ticket.

Our Support Hours

All response times are measured during our standard business hours:

9:00 AM – 5:00 PM U.S. Eastern Time, business days, excluding LinearB holidays

Time outside these hours does not count toward response time targets.

Your Role

  • You must enter all issues through the Zendesk portal. Issues reported only via email or during live working sessions will not be tracked as formal break/fix tickets and may not receive a timely response.
  • You are responsible for monitoring your open Zendesk tickets and responding to our requests for information.
  • If we need missing information, the SLA clock pauses until you respond in the ticket.
